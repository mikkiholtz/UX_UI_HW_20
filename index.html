Redesign a RWD website prototype for Energy.gov

Who uses Energy.gov & why?

The Energy.gov website needed to have a more clearly defined hierarchy of information on the needs of typical users.

Overview



After researching who our typical user may be, we discovered a majority of users navigate to DOE to research before purchasing plug-in electric and hybrid vehicles. Here they are able to gather information, data and discover nearby alternative fueling stations before making this investment.

  • My Role: UX/UI Designer
  • Type: Group Project
  • Team Members: Mikki Holtzhauer, Alan Donth, Jessica Santos
  • Time: 2 Weeks
  • Tools: Adobe XD, Invision, Figma, G-Suite, Powerpoint, Miro
  • Skills: Personas, Competitive Analysis, Wireframing, Prototyping, Usability Testing, Priority Matrix, Visual and User Interface Design, Heuristic Analysis, Color Accessibility Evaluation, Empathy Map, Card Sorting, Site Map, Mood Board, Style Tile, Brand Guide, Quantitative and Qualitative User Research, Data Analysis

    • Problem

    The Energy.gov website needed to have a more clearly defined hierarchy of information on the needs of typical users.

    Furthermore, the disorganized and text heavy website causes further confusion when navigating through the website which could potentially lead to frustrations and users exiting the page to use another resource.
